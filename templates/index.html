  • What is WikiLink?
  • WikiLink is a open source project that provides graph analysis over Wikipedia pages and categories, finding connections between things. It allows to search relationships in a tabular or graph way between different Wikipedia datasets.
    And it's funny!
  •  
  • Why have you done this?
  • We think that the chance people can see in a visual way how quite different subjects are correlated - from "GitHub" to "Germany" or "Cleopatra" to "SQL" - can provide some kind of knowledge and satisfaction. If you also believe so, help us to do this site better.
  •  
  • What we have
  •  
  • Check at bottom of the web the navigation menu. You can:
  •  
  • Search in the Wikipedia: Allow to navigate from Wikipedia categories & pages in a tabular way, having a quick index of whole Wikipedia at your reach.
  • Explore the Wikipedia: Have a big picture of how a single topic is co-related.
  • Graphing the Wikipedia: Search for a topic and swim deeply on its graph visualization.
  • Find the Shortest Path in Wikipedia: Find the shortest path to navigate from a Wikipedia page to another.
  • Contact WikiLink: Contact us with whatever inquiry or suggestion you have.
  • Licenses and ToS: Render therefore unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's.
  • Visit Wikipedia: Always is a good moment to spare some time with Wikipedia.
  •  
  • Where is the data coming from?
  • This site runs on a graph database - Neo4j - that has been populated with Wikipedia data for categories and pages through two methods: raw data bulk import or through the WikiMedia API.
